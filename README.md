## Why This Exists

The EU AI Act enters full enforcement on **August 2, 2026**. Most organizations deploying AI systems in or into the EU still don't know whether they are a Provider, Deployer, Importer, or Distributor — or what obligations each role carries.

This repository contains **text-based self-assessment versions** of every compliance tool available on [euaicompass.com/tools](https://euaicompass.com/tools.html). Each assessment can be completed with a pen and paper, a spreadsheet, or a text editor. No login. No data collection. No vendor lock-in.

**Want the interactive version?** Each tool links to its browser-based counterpart on [euaicompass.com](https://euaicompass.com), where all processing runs locally in your browser with zero server-side data collection.

## How to Use These Assessments

1. **Pick a starting point.** If you don't know whether the EU AI Act applies to you, start with Tool #1 (Quick Checker) or Tool #3 (Quick Risk Quiz).
2. **Work through the questions.** Each tool presents a series of questions with Yes/No/Not Sure answers. Record your answers in any format — paper, spreadsheet, or directly in the Markdown file.
3. **Interpret your results.** Each tool includes a scoring section that maps your answers to a compliance outcome, risk level, or action item list.
4. **Try the interactive version.** Every tool has a browser-based counterpart on [euaicompass.com](https://euaicompass.com/tools.html) that auto-calculates scores and generates exportable reports — all running locally in your browser with zero data collection.

## EU AI Act Compliance Topics Covered

This toolkit addresses the following compliance areas:

- **Risk classification** under Articles 5, 6, and Annex III (prohibited, high-risk, limited-risk, minimal-risk)
- **Provider obligations** under Articles 8–15 (quality management, technical documentation, conformity assessment, CE marking)
- **Deployer obligations** under Articles 26–29 (human oversight, input data quality, monitoring, incident reporting)
- **Transparency obligations** under Article 50 (chatbot disclosure, deepfake labelling, emotion recognition notice, synthetic content marking)
- **Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA)** under Article 27
- **Article 6(3) exemption** analysis and material influence evaluation
- **Accidental provider** reclassification under Article 25
- **GPAI model obligations** under Articles 51–56 and the Code of Practice
- **Sector-specific Annex III** requirements for biometrics, education, employment, finance, insurance, and industrial IoT
- **Governance framework alignment** with ISO 42001, ISO 27001, and NIST AI RMF
- **Shadow AI discovery** and unauthorized AI tool inventory
- **AI vendor risk** screening and DPA assessment
- **Agentic AI** autonomy boundaries and oversight requirements
- **RAG pipeline** data governance and Article 10 compliance
- **Bias testing** under Article 4a safe harbor provisions
- **AI content marking** under Article 50 Code of Practice Draft 2
